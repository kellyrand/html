Start Your Creative Business (The Right Way)

Hosted by Lisa Rowan of Beltway Vintage and Kelly Rand, author of Handmade to Sell

Have a great business idea but not sure what to do next? Get ready to work through common questions and misconceptions and start to think like a business. This interactive workshop will help you develop a clear picture of what it takes to start your creative business in Washington, DC. Think of it as a two-hour business boot camp, but with a softer kick in the pants.

In this two hours session you'll learn how to develop a product line, who your target audience is, how to price, the ins and outs of DCRA, budgeting and planning.

You'll also receive a light breakfast (bagels and unlimited coffee, of course), a custom Craftgasm notebook perfect for notes, business-planning worksheets, and your very own copy of Handmade to Sell to help you further your goals after the workshop.

Lisa Rowan is a freelance writer and editor with a background in local journalism. Beltway Vintage was a pop-up vintage shop called Quarter Life before moving into Monroe Street Market in 2013.

Kelly Rand is the author of Handmade to Sell: Hello Craft's Guide to Owning, Running, and Growing Your Crafty Biz. A freelance writer with a background in the Indie Craft community, Kelly believes that handmade will save the world.
